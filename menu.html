How we made your Espresso Special

  1. Heat the machine

    2. Turn on your espresso machine 15-20 mins before. Everything needs to be hot — group head, portafilter, cups. Cold metal ruins shots.

  2. Grind the beans

    3. Dose 18-20g of coffee for a double shot. Grind fine, like table salt. Too coarse = sour, fast shot. Too fine = bitter, choked shot.

  3. Distribute & tamp

    4. Put grounds in portafilter. Tap gently to level, then tamp straight down with 30 lbs pressure. You want a flat, even puck. No cracks.

  4. Purge the group head

    5. Run water 2 seconds before locking in portafilter. Clears old grounds and stabilizes temp.

  5. Lock in & brew immediately

    6. Insert portafilter, hit brew button right away. Delay = burnt coffee on hot group head.

  6. Time the extraction

    7. Aim for 25-30 seconds from first drip. You should get 36-40g liquid out from 18-20g coffee. That’s 1:2 ratio.

  7. Watch the shot

    8. First 5 sec: dark, thick. Then it goes caramel. Last 5 sec: lighter, “blonding”. Stop before it goes pale yellow.

  8. Serve immediately

    9. Espresso dies fast. Serve within 30 seconds. Crema should be thick, reddish-brown, no big bubbles.

Quick troubleshooting:

  • Shot pulled in 15 sec → Grind finer or tamp harder
  • Shot took 40+ sec → Grind coarser or dose less
  • Sour taste → Under-extracted. Grind finer or brew longer
  • Bitter taste → Over-extracted. Grind coarser or stop sooner

This is what we train every barista at Aurora on. Dialing in the grind daily is 90% of good espresso.
